Heart Rate Monitoring

Pulse sensors can be used for general health monitoring, especially for individuals with heart conditions or those at risk of cardiovascular diseases. Continuous heart rate monitoring can provide insights into heart health and help detect abnormalities or irregularities in the heart rate.

 

In medical settings, pulse sensors can be used for monitoring patients' heart rates during procedures, surgeries, or in critical care situations. They provide real-time data to healthcare professionals, aiding in the assessment and management of patients' conditions.

Electro CardioGram

ECG is commonly used to diagnose various heart conditions, such as arrhythmias (abnormal heart rhythms), myocardial infarction (heart attack), ischemia (reduced blood flow to the heart), heart valve disorders, and congenital heart defects. The ECG waveform provides insights into the electrical abnormalities or disruptions in the heart's normal functioning.

ECG is used for continuous monitoring of patients' heart activity, especially in critical care units, during cardiac procedures, or after a heart attack. It provides real-time information about any changes in the heart's electrical activity, allowing healthcare professionals to respond promptly to any emergent situations.

Body & Room Temperature

Wireless sensors equipped with temperature probes can be placed on patients' bodies to continuously monitor their temperature. These sensors can be integrated into wearable devices or adhesive patches. The sensors collect temperature data and transmit it to a central monitoring system using IoT connectivity.

 

IoT-enabled temperature sensors can be installed in different areas of hospital rooms, including patient rooms, operating rooms, and storage areas, to monitor the ambient temperature.

Saline Levels

Remote saline level monitoring in hospitals can be achieved using IoT (Internet of Things) technology combined with sensors and a centralized monitoring system.

This enables healthcare staff to monitor and manage saline levels in real-time,

ensuring an adequate supply for patient care.

 

Saline level sensors are installed in the saline storage containers, such as IV bags, saline solution bottles, or storage tanks. These sensors can be based on different technologies, such as pressure sensors or capacitive sensors, that measure the fluid level.

 

Emergency Notifications

Emergency alert notifications in hospitals are crucial for quickly communicating critical information to the relevant staff members and ensuring a prompt response to emergencies.

 

Code Blue alerts are triggered to notify the medical team about a patient experiencing a cardiac arrest or other life-threatening emergencies. These alerts typically include the location of the emergency, allowing nearby medical staff to respond immediately.

 

Code Red alerts are used to alert staff members about fire emergencies. These notifications provide information about the location of the fire, evacuation protocols, and other necessary instructions to ensure a swift and orderly response.
